Introduction

ICMonster, founded in 2017, is a fun-filled café located in San Diego, California. ICMonster strives to make delicious tea beverages - including milk tea, bubble tea, and specialty monster drinks - from only natural ingredients. In addition to brewing a variety of whimsical tea drinks, ICMonster also doubles as a rolled ice cream shop. Specializing in crafting delectable Thai Rolled Ice Cream, ICMonster is capable of satiating any sweet tooth's cravings. Snack on some soft serve, nibble on one of ICMonster's rolled ice cream egg waffle tacos or sip on a housemade monstar drink. Whether you're here with friends or on the go, ICMonster's drinks and rolled ice creams are sure to satisfy.

Who is this company’s competitors?

For ice cream, their main competitors are Bing Haus and Pacific Knots. For teas, examples would be Ding Tea, Sharetea, and any other boba shop in the San Diego area.

What sets ICMonstar apart from competitors?

ICMonstar’s drinks are less sweet than other drink places. A 100% sugar level is other shops’ 75% sugar. For example, our winter melon drink only uses 1 pump of syrup versus 2 pumps in other places, which led to some customer complaints about weak flavoring or unexpected coloring in “Monster” drinks. We are healthier and more natural with no use of artificial coloring. Our teas are based off of those made by Oo Tea, since an employee of Oo Tea was actually the one who taught us how to make boba. They are creamier and use better quality teas compared to other chain boba stores. We also had cheese cream topping before Happy Lemon and mini boba, but it was hard to promote these add-ons as a local store (non-chain).



Design Research & User Needs

ICMonster gets a lot of families with kids and many students. There aren’t a lot of people over age 40 unless they have kids. It’s mostly a younger crowd. We are slowly growing towards more mature kids like teenagers and college students. Our location in Convoy is already being revamped for a more mature demographic.

How do they retain customers?

ICMonster uses the Loyalty app because it’s cheaper than Fivestars, which wants $600 per month. Before, they used stamp cards.

How are they attracting customers?

They created a “Weekend Happy Hour,” where they use LED lights and offer drinks only at a flat discounted rate to attract college-aged clientele, transforming their stores into more of a hang-out spot like Kung Fu Tea. They are also looking into implementing new trends, such as LED ice cubes and dry ice. The website could be a good way to attract customers as well to show them what we have.


Wireframes

Our prototype required us to have a website that is functional and aesthetic. The layout had to be simple in personal ways that would communicate to the user that they should type their name and discover how it is translated or transliterated in multiple languages. There needed to be a title, text box, and a statement addressing users to type their name in the text box. Our solution was simple and it clearly communicated to users to enter their name.
