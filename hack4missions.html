Introduction

The Bible is one of the oldest and most popular books of all time. It’s something that everyone should be able to read in their own language, but approximately 1,600 languages around the world are still waiting for a translation project to begin. The purpose of this project by Wycliffe Bible Translators is "to help speakers of these remaining languages get the Bible for themselves. And we won’t stop until all people have God’s Word in a language they understand."



Design Research & User Needs

This web app helps individuals visualize the diversity of language and the value of Bible translation. It allows users from different countries to type their names and see them instantly translated or transliterated into several languages. It utilizes an interactive React dashboard used by missionaries and churches around the world and also at Wycliffe's Discovery Center located in Orlando, Florida.


Design Requirements & Solution

Our prototype required us to have a website that is functional and aesthetic. The layout had to be simple in personal ways that would communicate to the user that they should type their name and discover how it is translated or transliterated in multiple languages. There needed to be a title, text box, and a statement addressing users to type their name in the text box. Our solution was simple and it clearly communicated to users to enter their name.
