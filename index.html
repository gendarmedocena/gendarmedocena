About Myself

I wasn't always the type to pursue a career related to software. I've always been the one to play sports, watch sports, and learn the ways to fix sports injuries. I wanted to be a Physical Therapist. But as I grew up in Bay Area and witnessed the splurge technology (such as the rise of iPhones, Tesla apps, FitBits, Alexas, etc.) all around me, I had to learn how these products were developed. I wanted to be part of this technological-upbringing, which has brought me through 3 years at community college & 1 year at UC San Diego. And here I am today, wrapping up my undergraduate experience with a major in Cognitive Science (specialization in Human-Computer Interaction)!

While my passion is building and designing software, I want to connect with people because the products I develop is ultimately for them! And by doing that, I learned photography. Photography helps me engage with individuals by understanding what images appeal and don't appeal to them. I step into their unique lenses by learning their reactions & responses toward colors, tones, contrasts, exposures, and all the photos I take. Through this, I gain stronger insight on how to build software. Now, I recognize the needs our communities desire and look forward to the opportunities in applying these human factor principles!
